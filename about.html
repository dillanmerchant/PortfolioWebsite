Hello!

One of Dillan's main ideologies stems from his Dad, who believes that one should always live their life to the fullest and not take it for granted. As a result, Dillan is always seeking new opportunities and trying to step out of his comfort zone to gain new perspectives. He believes that the experiences and challenges that one goes through in life shapes who they are and enables them to grow as a person. In the words of C.S. Lewis, "Experience: that most brutal of teachers. But you learn, my God do you learn."

Growing up in San Diego, he is greatful for the opportunities he has been given and attributes a lot of his values and character to his parents.

Currently he is pursuing a Computer Science degree from the Jacobs School of Engineering at UCSD. He believes the experience he gains from this field will be very beneficial and relevant in today's technologically advancing society. Dance
Dancing is one of Dillan's greatest passions. At a young age, he remembers swaying to the music that his parents used to play. He started pursuing his passion in sophmore year of high school when he joined the B-boy breakdancing team. In junior and senior year, he used his dance skills to become choreographer and president of a student-run organization called South Asian Culture Show. SACS included close to 150 students from 10 high schools across San Diego and its primary goal was to raise money for charities in South Asia. The year that Dillan was president, the club raised close to $10,000. Finally, his dance journey continued in college when he joined the competetive Bollywood Fusion dance team known as Zor. Zor managed to place 5th in the nation through dedication and hard work.

Tennis
After casually playing tennis with his dad on weekends, Dillan became interested in this sport in 7th grade. On his first team, he played #1 singles and won the USTA sectionals. Gaining confidence with this experience, he made the Varisty tennis team in high school and became assistant coach for On the Rise Tennis Academy. He is still coaching kids in tennis today.

Dog Rescue
Dillan's love for dogs cultivated after babysitting a friend's maltese. This motivated him to get involved in a small local rescue called Holly's Garden. For the last few years, his family has fostered many dogs until each dog found a permanent home. Dillan has helped with fundraising, managing social media, and maintaining the website for Holly's Garden. As he gains more technical skills from his CS major at UCSD, he hopes to continue finding innovative ways to help dogs in need.
